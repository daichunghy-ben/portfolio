EV Choice Experiment in Vietnam

This consumer insights study shows how young Vietnamese consumers trade off battery range, charging times, price, and design when choosing their next EV.

Hanoi & HCMC, Vietnam 212 Validated Shoppers Choice Experiment

The Core Challenge

This applied market research study asks a simple question: when EVs are also status symbols, how much range do young Vietnamese buyers trade for design and convenience?

Range Anxiety vs Style

Range and charging times are major barriers to EV adoption. But does a beautifully designed car make buyers more forgiving of those trade-offs?

Two Types of Buyers

The market splits into two groups: Pragmatic Adopters (65%) and Aesthetic Enthusiasts (35%).

Putting a Price on Looks

By capturing their choices, we estimated how much extra money consumers are willing to pay for a streamlined, sporty-looking EV.

Key Discoveries

The Business Reality

  • Function still rules: Higher prices immediately turn away buyers, and long range is the ultimate deal-maker.
  • Aesthetics are a premium lever: For 35% of the market, a sleek design can justify slightly higher prices or slower charging times.
  • No one-size-fits-all: Marketing a single "average" EV is dangerous. Brands must pick whether they are selling to the wallet or the heart.
