Do consumers value EV design alongside range?

A stated-choice study estimating how a sample of young Vietnamese shoppers weighed range, charging time, infrastructure, price, and exterior design. Findings are model-based estimates, not a census of the Vietnamese EV market.

Hanoi & HCMC, Vietnam Sample: n = 212 shoppers Stated choice + latent classes
Project brief

Research framing and evidence limits

Method in brief

Respondents evaluated EV profiles with controlled differences in price, driving range, charging time, charging-station availability, and design profile. Choice data were estimated with a preference model and summarized through two latent classes.

Evidence caveat

Percentages and willingness-to-pay values should be read as sample/model estimates. They describe relative trade-offs in this experiment and should not be presented as national demand, guaranteed revenue, or exact consumer prices.

Research Question

The study asks whether exterior design has measurable value after accounting for functional EV attributes. The focus is not whether design overrides utility, but whether design adds a statistically visible premium within a controlled choice setting.

Utility First

Range, charging time, infrastructure, and price remain the core EV adoption attributes. The design variable is interpreted alongside these functional constraints, not in isolation.

Estimated Preference Classes

The latent-class model suggests two preference patterns in this sample: a utility-focused class (65%) and a design-sensitive class (35%). Treat the split as an estimated sample structure.

Estimated WTP for Design

Choice coefficients are converted into marginal willingness-to-pay estimates. The values describe modeled trade-offs for a streamlined EV profile, not exact prices any individual will pay.

Key Findings

Evidence-Led Readout

  • Utility remains the anchor: Higher price reduces utility, while longer range increases choice probability in the estimated model.
  • Design is an additional cue: The design-sensitive class shows positive model-estimated WTP for a streamlined EV profile.
  • Segment claims need caution: The two-class split is an estimated preference structure for this sample, not a fixed national market share.
