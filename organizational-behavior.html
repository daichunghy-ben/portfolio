Organizational Behavior Research

This cluster covers the organizational side of the portfolio: psychological ownership in universities and co-creation in participatory service settings. What this topic is for

Understanding how commitment and participation are shaped by ownership, social value, and co-created experience rather than by instruction alone.

Typical questionsHow do teams or participants feel more invested, and which service or organizational cues make repeat participation more likely?
Common methodsOwnership frameworks, SEM-style reasoning, service-design translation, and participatory-value modeling.
Decision valueThe output supports program design, engagement strategy, and commitment-building in educational or experiential contexts.
What I Look For

Conditions that make people invest themselves.

The work in this cluster centers on why people choose to stay involved, contribute, and identify with a setting or experience instead of treating engagement as a purely motivational issue.

Ownership as a leverPeople commit more deeply when they feel a meaningful stake in what they are contributing to or participating in.
Experience as a systemLearning, social value, emotional value, and shared creation often work together rather than as isolated drivers.
Translation to practiceThese pages interpret frameworks for concrete institutional or service-design use rather than leaving them abstract.
