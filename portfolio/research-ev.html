EV Choice Experiment in Vietnam

A discrete choice experiment exploring how young Vietnamese shoppers trade off price, performance, design, and convenience when choosing an EV.

1-minute takeaway

What a reviewer should take away first.

Problem EV teams need to know which product attributes young Vietnamese buyers value most: range, charging, price, and design.
Sample 212 validated young consumers in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
Method Stated-choice experiment with attribute trade-off interpretation.
Main finding Two clear segments prioritize range and price, while design-sensitive buyers demand emotional and functional trade-offs.
Use Segment EV messaging and product bundles instead of marketing one average vehicle profile.

The core challenge

How much range do buyers trade for design and convenience?

This applied market research study asks how young Vietnamese buyers balance EV utility, price, charging friction, and visible style.

Range anxiety vs styleRange and charging remain major barriers, but design can shift perceived trade-off tolerance.
Two types of buyersThe model splits into pragmatic adopters (65%) and aesthetic enthusiasts (35%).
Putting a price on looksChoices estimate how much extra buyers accept for a streamlined, sporty-looking EV.
