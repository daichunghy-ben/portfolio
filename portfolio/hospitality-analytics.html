Scope

This topic examines guest differences, rating penalties, and service actions derived from experience signals.

Typical questions Which guests should a hotel prioritize, which attributes create the largest downside risk, and how should text data be translated into value-led action?
Common methods Aspect-based sentiment analysis, clustering, Sentence-BERT mapping, PRCA, Kano, and value interpretation frameworks.
Decision value The work supports prioritization, positioning, and service-quality diagnostics rather than generic dashboard reporting.
Analytical focus

Operationally useful patterns

The hospitality pages focus on differences rather than averages. They show where guest expectations separate, where hotel attributes behave asymmetrically, and how review text can inform action.

Guest heterogeneity Hotel rating averages flatten distinct segments. Segmentation is used here to recover meaningful guest patterns from review text.
Penalty and reward logic Some hotel attributes matter more when they fail than when they succeed. That asymmetry matters for operational prioritization.
Translation to action Framework pages are included when they help convert raw evidence into service, value, and positioning decisions.
