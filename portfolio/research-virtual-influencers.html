The Core Challenge

The Problem

Virtual influencers are effective because of novelty, but marketers lacked evidence on the cues that truly drive Gen Z purchase intention.

The Method

Simulated Instagram posts manipulated argument quality and peripheral appeal across four scenarios, then tested effects on trust and purchase intent.

The Result

Peripheral cues drive purchase intention more consistently than argument quality, while speciesism weakens persuasion for skeptical consumers.

Speciesism Moderates the Effect

High-speciesism participants remain less persuaded across conditions.

Interpretation

As speciesism increases, the positive effect of persuasive cues on purchase intention weakens. Strong styling and relatability cannot fully convert skeptical consumers.

Key Takeaway

Speciesism acts as a ceiling. Even strong cues cannot fully unlock high-speciesism consumers.

Design Implications for Brands

Leverage Peripheral Cues

Invest in visual appeal, likability, and value alignment to drive Gen Z purchase intention.

Build Relatable AI Identities

Strengthen attitude homophily through shared interests, tone, and community values.

Quality Still Matters

Do not ignore argument quality. It builds credibility and supports long-term trust.

Mind the Speciesism Ceiling

Address skepticism through transparency, authenticity, and human-compatible storytelling.
