Why feeling "mine" or "ours" at work matters for universities.

We explored how psychological ownership shapes commitment and teamwork in Vietnamese universities--and where local evidence is still emerging.

01 / Plain-language decoder

The idea, simply put.

Ownership is not only legal control. In universities, it is the felt sense that work, identity, and outcomes are partly "mine" or "ours."

It starts with "mine"

People can shape the work and see themselves in it.

It builds connection

Care for the work can become commitment.

It creates "ours"

Shared responsibility supports trust and identity.

It improves outcomes

Better commitment can lift service and student experience.

02 / Evidence confidence

What we know -- and what we're still learning.

01Strong Evidence

Core ownership ideas and links to commitment are well supported globally and locally.

02Moderate Evidence

Commitment pathway works, but the effect size varies by context.

03Missing Local Evidence

Specific mechanisms and effect sizes in Vietnamese universities are not yet confirmed.
