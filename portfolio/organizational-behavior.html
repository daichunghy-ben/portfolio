Organizational Behavior Projects

This cluster covers psychological ownership in universities and co-creation in participatory service settings. It groups work on commitment, involvement, and repeat participation.

Scope

This topic examines how commitment and participation are shaped by ownership, social value, and co-created experience.

Typical questionsHow do teams or participants become more invested, and which service or organizational cues make repeat participation more likely?
Common methodsOwnership frameworks, SEM-style reasoning, service-design translation, and participatory-value modeling.
Decision valueThe output supports program design, engagement strategy, and commitment-building in educational or experiential contexts.
Analytical focus

Conditions that make people invest themselves.

This cluster examines why people remain involved, contribute, and identify with a setting or experience.

Ownership as a leverPeople commit more deeply when they feel a meaningful stake in what they are contributing to or participating in.
Experience as a systemLearning, social value, emotional value, and shared creation often work together rather than as isolated drivers.
Translation to practiceThese pages translate frameworks into institutional and service-design applications.
