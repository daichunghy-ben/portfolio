Virtual Influencer Trust

The most useful way to read the virtual influencer study is to treat trust and credibility as the gating variables. Purchase intention does not move first. It tends to follow when the virtual creator feels believable, coherent, and contextually fit for the audience.

Key Takeaways

Three ways to interpret the trust signal

The research is most useful when it separates surface novelty from the cues that make a virtual influencer persuasive enough to affect intent.

Credibility comes before conversion If the virtual influencer does not feel credible, purchase intention is unlikely to move in a durable way regardless of novelty or aesthetics.
Audience fit matters as much as the asset itself Gen Z response depends on whether the creator cue feels native to the audience context, not just technologically impressive.
Trust is a platform strategy issue, not just a character issue The surrounding content environment, message framing, and brand fit all shape how the virtual influencer is interpreted.
Decision Use

Why this matters for creator-economy strategy.

This insight is most valuable when translated into creator strategy, audience segmentation, and brand-fit decisions rather than novelty-driven experimentation.

For creator strategy review

Trust requires explicit design Virtual creators require a believable identity, coherent behavior, and platform-fit cues before they can support brand or commerce goals.
Novelty is not the same as persuasion The character may attract attention, but trust and contextual credibility are what make downstream intent plausible.
