Decoding Hotel Reviews

A self-reported Da Nang hotel study using 74,896 Booking.com reviews to derive 10 review-based guest profiles from aspect-level sentiment and clustering.

Da Nang, Vietnam 74,896 Reviews 458 Hotels Clustering & Sentiment Setup
Project Brief

Review-Led Guest Segmentation

The page presents an internal research portfolio study, not a public benchmark dataset. Its role is to show how review text can be converted into segment hypotheses that help hotel teams compare guest priorities before designing service interventions.

Evidence Status

Self-reported

Reported Scope

74,896 reviews / 458 hotels

Analytical Unit

Review-derived guest profiles

The Core Challenge

Aggregate scores can combine very different guest experiences into one average. A cleanliness complaint, a location preference, and a value concern point to different operational responses, so the review text needs to be read at a more granular level.

The Problem

Summary ratings do not show which guest groups are raising which service issues, making prioritization harder for hotel teams.

The Method

The workflow maps aspect-level sentiment from reviews, then uses clustering to form guest profiles with shared experience patterns.

The Result

The reported output is 10 review-derived profiles that can guide service hypotheses, messaging priorities, and further validation.

Visual Evidence

Evidence Reading

  • The reported segmentation separates review patterns into profiles such as Minimalists and Experience Seekers.
  • Staff and location appear as broad experience signals, while value and comfort help differentiate more dissatisfied profiles.
  • Interpretation: the visuals support operational hypothesis-building; they should not be read as causal effect estimates.
