Decoding Hotel Reviews

Transforming 74,896 unstructured Booking.com reviews in Da Nang into an actionable 10-profile customer segmentation using aspect-based sentiment analysis.

Da Nang, Vietnam 74,896 Reviews Clustering & Sentiment Setup

The Core Challenge

Aggregated ratings (e.g., "8.2/10") hide critical operational nuances. A "Cleanliness" complaint from an older couple differs entirely from a solo traveler's priority on "Location". Hotel teams need granular experience profiles.

The Problem

Aggregated scores mask the specific drivers of dissatisfaction among different guest segments, making prioritized operational changes impossible.

The Method

An NLP pipeline mapped aspect-level sentiment followed by validated clustering algorithms to group travelers based on shared concerns.

The Result

10 distinct consumer profiles, allowing hoteliers to tailor their service interventions to the precise expectations of their most frequent guests.

Visual Evidence

Diagnostics Summary

  • The text data clearly clustered, distinguishing groups like "Minimalists" from "Experience Seekers".
  • Aspects like "Staff" and "Location" drove universal satisfaction, while "Value" and "Comfort" were key differentiators for unhappy clusters.
  • Conclusion: NLP models built on raw review text can rapidly identify service failures across different personas.
