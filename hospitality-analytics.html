What this topic is for

Understanding how guests differ, what causes rating penalties, and how experience signals can be translated into clearer service actions.

Typical questions Which guests should a hotel prioritize, which attributes create the largest downside risk, and how should text data be translated into value-led action?
Common methods Aspect-based sentiment analysis, clustering, Sentence-BERT mapping, PRCA, Kano, and value interpretation frameworks.
Decision value The work is aimed at teams that need prioritization, positioning, or service-quality diagnostics instead of generic dashboard reporting.
What I Look For

Patterns that are operationally useful.

The hospitality pages in this portfolio are structured around difference, not averages. The goal is to show where guest expectations separate, where hotel attributes behave asymmetrically, and where text can be turned into action rather than description.

Guest heterogeneity Hotel rating averages flatten distinct segments. Segmentation is used here to recover meaningful guest patterns from review text.
Penalty and reward logic Some hotel attributes matter more when they fail than when they succeed. That asymmetry matters for operational prioritization.
Translation to action Framework pages are included when they help convert raw evidence into more practical service, value, and positioning moves.
