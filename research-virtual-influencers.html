Virtual Influencer Trust and Purchase Intention

This consumer insights study explores how virtual influencer cues shape Generation Z trust, credibility, and purchase intention.

Simulated Environments Springer Publishing published ANOVA Testing

The Core Challenge

With the rise of VTubers and photorealistic CGI models, brands needed clearer evidence on what makes a virtual influencer persuasive to a Gen Z audience.

The Problem

Are virtual influencers effective because of novelty, or are there deeper trust levers at play? Marketers lacked evidence on the boundary limits.

The Method

Constructed a survey model testing direct and mediated relationships based on simulated Instagram scenarios.

The Result

Speciesism acts as a moderator. Credibility and argument quality outrank pure aesthetic novelty in this Gen Z purchase-intention study.

Verified Output:

Springer chapter published online June 18, 2025 (DOI: 10.1007/978-981-96-4116-1_9).

Visual Evidence & Test Environment

Simulation Blocks & Findings

  • The Visual Test: We built 4 different Instagram interface mockups to test high vs. low argument quality combined with high vs. low aesthetics.
  • The Findings: Pretty visuals (peripheral cues) cannot save a bad product pitch (central cue). Gen Z fundamentally evaluates digital entities functionally.
  • If a user possesses high "speciesism" (bias against robots/AI), the virtual influencer's impact hits a strict upper ceiling.
