Methodology

How I usually structure applied work.

The specific method changes by topic, but the general workflow stays consistent: frame the decision, design the evidence, translate results into action, and label uncertainty honestly.

1. Frame the decision clearly I prioritize questions that a hiring manager, operations lead, or project owner could actually use to make a choice.
2. Use methods that fit the question The portfolio includes sentiment analysis, clustering, discrete choice experiments, structural equation modeling, regression, and decision translation.
3. Report with evidence discipline I separate verified publication output, self-reported project work, derived interpretation, and conceptual framing instead of blending them into one claim set.
